PCA/NCA Preparation

Drug Dosage
Morphine 1 mg/kg
Fentanyl 25 mcg/kg
Oxycodone 1 mg/kg
Ketamine 1 mg/kg
Ketamine (Age under 1 year) 0.5 mg/kg


NCA < 5 Suggested Range
Continuous 0.2 ml/hr 0 - 0.5 ml/hr
Bolus 0.5 ml 0.5 ml
Lockout 30 min 15 - 30 min


NCA > 5 Suggested Range
Continuous 0.5 ml/hr 0 - 1 ml/hr
Bolus 1 ml 0.5 - 1 ml
Lockout 20 min 20 - 30 min


PCA Suggested Range
Continuous 0.2 ml/hr 0 - 1 ml/hr
Bolus 1 ml 1 ml
Lockout 5 min 5 - 10 min

Lower Limb

Less than 4 years old - single shot caudal and NCA overnight (usually go in a hip spica after the procedure)

More than 4 years old - lumbar epidural catheter or ultrasound guided fascia iliaca or quadratus lumborum block, can then give PRN oramorph or opioid NCA

Local anaesthetic infiltration by the surgeon to mobilise patients immediately post-surgery so they can weight-bear on the affected leg.

USG Fascia Iliaca (FI) block +/- catheter

Either epidural or USG QL catheter or USG FI and sciatic nerve perineural catheters

Small pieces of metal and small incisions so LA infiltration by surgeon only

Adductor canal block and local from the surgeon, or consider iPack block, if ligaments/meniscal work

Proximal tibia - either epidural, USG QL or FI and sciatic nerve perineural catheter

Distal tibia - high USG popliteal sciatic perineural catheter and adductor canal

Less than 2 years old - Caudal block

More than 2 years old - USG popliteal sciatic and adductor canal blocks

The surgeon should site wound infiltration catheter with the appropriate length and LA delivered by elastomeric pump for 24-36 hours.

Epidural catheter, QL catheter or FI and Sciatic catheters for 48-72 hours

Upper Limb

USG interscalene/ supraclavicular brachial plexus (BP) block

USG interscalene/ supraclavicular / infraclavicular BP block

Can be very painful, discuss with the surgeon whether they are happy for you to do a single-shot BP block. If not, give morphine and NCA, please.

USG axillary BP block +/- catheter depending on the likely length of stay

For some hand surgeries, USG single shot blocks of median, radial and ulnar nerves below the elbow in the forearm. Surgeons also like to do their own blocks, so always discuss with them an intra and post-op analgesia plan.

Fractures are immobilised in casts, blocks generally aren't indicated, paracetamol and ibuprofen are sufficient.

Urology

USG paravertebral block or erector spinae block and regular post-operative oral analgesia

Less than 2 years old: caudal or penile block

More than 2 years old: penile block

Distal hypospadias either caudal or penile block, Proximal hypospadias caudal or penile and pudendal nerve block to cover the base of the penis.

Can involve freeing the penile soft tissue right down onto the scrotum - caudal block is most effective.

Laparoscopic formation - oral analgesia only

Open Surgery - usually midline vertical incision from just above the umbilicus to symphysis pubis. Rectus sheath catheters by surgeons and NCA /PCA

Small bowel harvested and used to augment bladder size. Vertical incision from above umbilicus to symphysis pubis - Rectus sheath catheters by surgeon and NCA/PCA.

Oral analgesia only post-op

Consider caudal or bilateral TAP blocks

General Surgery

USG Ilio-inguinal supra-iliac crest TAP or lateral QL block (plus scrotal incision infiltration by surgeon). Consider single shot caudal block for <2 years and if bilateral procedure.

USG Ilio-inguinal TAP or lateral QL block. Consider caudal for <2 years and if bilateral procedure.

Children with significant cognitive impairment do well with just LA infiltration in port sites by surgeon. For older and developmentally normal patients consider unilateral subcostal TAP block for gastrostomy site as well as infiltration by surgeon in port wounds. Per Gastrostomy analgesia post-op- possible straight away on return to the ward.

Oral analgesia post-op.

USG TAP block rectus sheath or caudal and then NCA morphine or PR codeine for infants <5kg.

Infants <10 kg – single catheter in pre-peritoneal layer. Patients >10kg - rectus sheath catheters placed under direct vision by surgeons and then PCA/NCA.

USG TAP if wound on side of stoma but if incision extends across midline do bilateral TAP or add in rectus sheath block plus PCA/NCA morphine or PR codeine for infants < 5kg.

Plastic Surgery

Discuss with surgeon whether they do wrist block or want you to do axillary BP block or USG median ulnar and radial nerve blocks in forearm.

USG BP block.

USG pec blocks.

Isolated cleft lip – infraorbital nerve blocks by surgeon simple analgesia post-op sufficient.

Lip and vomerine flap – IO by surgeon paracetamol ibuprofen and oramorph post-op on ward.

Cleft palate repair – infiltration +/- blocks by surgeon intra-op paracetamol ibuprofen and oramorph post-op.

Pharyngoplasty – post-op paracetamol ibuprofen and oramorph. Consider NCA first night if patient refusing oral meds.

Buccinator flap – surgeon infiltrates cheek and palate. Regular paracetamol and ibuprofen prn oramorph.

Alveolar Bone Graft to palatal fistula – Iliac crest bone graft harvested to fill palatal fistula. Surgeon will site a wound infiltration catheter as closing iliac wound for overnight infusion of LA via elastomeric pump. Oral analgesia sufficient for palatal wound.

ENT

Regular paracetamol + ibuprofen (if no contraindication) and then prn oramorph 0.1mg/kg 6 hourly as TTO. If patients has severe OSA then only oramorph 0.05mg/kg as TTO or occasionally no oramorph at all if still significant obstructive symptoms post-op.

Surgeon infiltrates and blocks auricular nerves so post-op paracetamol +/- ibuprofen.

Surgeon infiltrates regular paracetamol and ibuprofen afterwards suffices.

Local infiltration by surgeon and oral analgesia usually sufficient.
